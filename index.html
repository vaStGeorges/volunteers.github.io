Church Breakfast Program

Volunteer Role Descriptions

Welcome, valued volunteer! This guide is designed to provide a clear and interactive overview of the essential roles that make our breakfast program a success. Please select a role below to view its specific responsibilities. Your dedication helps create a warm and welcoming experience for everyone.

Coffee/Tea Station Team: Breakfast Prep & Beverage Leads

This role focuses on preparation, staging, and managing all beverages and food staging prior to service, ensuring everything is ready for the servers.

Key Responsibilities:

  • ☕Beverage Management: Verify Brewing Tea and coffee started on time. Manage large quantities of hot tea (for children) and hot tea and coffee (for adults).
  • 🌡️Temperature Control: Ensure the children’s tea is brewed to a safe, lukewarm temperature or pre-cooled for immediate consumption to prevent any accidental burns. **Pre-Mix appropriate level of sugar**.
  • 🤝Clergy Coordination: Coordinate with the clergy food preparers to receive the pre-cut bread and stage it at the designated serving stations.
  • 📦Supply Staging: Set up all necessary serving supplies (e.g., creamers, sugar, spoons, napkins, trays) near the respective serving areas for both children and adults.
  • 🔄Replenishment: Monitor tea and coffee levels during breakfast time and refills as needed.

Role 2: Floor Supervisor & Transition Team

This expanded role is critical, focusing first on managing children's seating and safety, and then quickly transitioning the dining space for adult service.

Children's Supervision Responsibilities:

  • 👋Greeting & Seating: Greet children as they arrive in the dining area and guide them efficiently to their seats at the tables.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦Crowd Control: Maintain order around the tables before and during the meal, ensuring children remain seated until dismissed.
  • 👀Supervision: Actively supervise the children while they eat, watching for any safety issues (spills, conflicts, or choking hazards).
  • 🙋Needs Assistance: Be the first point of contact for children who need help (e.g., retrieving dropped food, needing a napkin, or assistance with seating).
  • ➡️Flow Coordination: Coordinate with the Service Team (Role 3) to ensure only seated children are served.

Adult Transition & Service Responsibilities:

  • 🗑️Table Clearing: Immediately after the children have been dismissed, clear all remaining cups, napkins, and debris from the children’s tables.
  • ✨Venue Transition: Fold and stack the children’s dining tables and chairs, or relocate them to the designated storage area/adult setup area, ensuring the space is ready for the adults.

Role 3: Children’s Meal Server & Communicator (The Service Team /Young Volunteers)

This role provides direct meal service and handles all necessary verbal communication and announcements via the microphone.

Key Responsibilities:

  • 🥤Setup: Place disposable cups at each spot on the children’s tables immediately before children are seated.
  • 🍞Service: Distribute pre-cut bread to the seated children.
  • 💧Pouring: Pour the pre-prepared, safe-temperature tea into the children's cups.
  • 🎤Mic Communication (Pre-Meal): Briefly and clearly announce the serving process and general rules (e.g., "Stay seated until you finish breakfast").
  • 📢Mic Communication (Dismissal): Operate the microphone to clearly announce when children are dismissed to their classes or next activities once they are finished eating.

Role 5: Volunteer Coordinator

This key administrative role manages the recruitment and preparedness of youth helpers, ensuring a strong and well-informed team is ready to serve the children.

Key Responsibilities:

  • 📝Youth Sign-ups: Initiate and manage the sign-up process for youth helpers to serve breakfast for the younger children.
  • 📚Volunteer Orientation: Conduct a mandatory orientation session for all registered youth volunteers to review safety rules, serving expectations, and role responsibilities before their first shift.
  • 🗓️Scheduling Support: Assist in creating a consistent schedule that aligns youth helper availability with the breakfast service needs.

🔔 Breakfast Time Rules

  • Walk quietly from the church to the fellowship hall.
  • Put your **shoes on** when you enter the hall.
  • Wait patiently until you are served your bread and tea.
  • Be respectful to those who serve you, remember to say **“please” and “thank you.”**
  • Do **not** line up to get tea or sugar. Volunteers will serve you.
  • Once you receive your tea and bread, do not go back for more; think about others who haven't been served yet.
  • Remember, you are in God’s house. Show **love and respect** to everyone.
  • Use kind words, no offensive or hurtful language.
  • Keep an open heart and mind. Make new friends and include others, even those you don’t know well.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Fellowship Hall Guidelines

These rules are specifically for our **young volunteers** and all children to ensure safety and order in the Fellowship Hall before, during, and after service.

  • Only **assigned volunteers** should go to the fellowship hall after finishing mezmur and Bible reading, if assigned that day.
  • Any kids who come to the fellowship hall **before church service ends** must be accompanied by a parent.
  • No one is allowed in the fellowship hall **before prayer service ends**.
  • Only **adult volunteers** are allowed at the coffee and refreshment tables.
