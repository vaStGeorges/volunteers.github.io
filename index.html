Church Breakfast Program

Volunteer Role Descriptions

Welcome, valued volunteer! This guide is designed to provide a clear and interactive overview of the essential roles that make our breakfast program a success. Please select a role below to view its specific responsibilities. Your dedication helps create a warm and welcoming experience for everyone.

Role 1: Breakfast Prep & Beverage Lead (The Kitchen Team)

This role focuses on preparation, staging, and managing all beverages and food staging prior to service, ensuring everything is ready for the servers.

Key Responsibilities:

  • ☕Beverage Management: Brew and manage large quantities of hot tea (for children) and hot tea and coffee (for adults).
  • 🌡️Temperature Control: Ensure the children’s tea is brewed to a safe, lukewarm temperature or pre-cooled for immediate consumption to prevent any accidental burns.
  • 🤝Clergy Coordination: Coordinate with the clergy food preparers to receive the pre-cut bread and stage it at the designated serving stations.
  • 📦Supply Staging: Set up all necessary serving supplies (e.g., creamers, sugar, spoons, napkins, trays) near the respective serving areas for both children and adults.
  • 🔄Replenishment: Monitor tea and coffee levels during service and quickly brew/prepare refills as needed.

Role 2: Children’s Seating & Safety Guide (The Floor Supervisor)

This role focuses on managing the flow of children, maintaining a safe environment, and ensuring kids are seated quickly and orderly.

Key Responsibilities:

  • 👋Greeting & Seating: Greet children as they arrive in the dining area and guide them efficiently to their seats at the tables.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦Crowd Control: Maintain order around the tables before and during the meal, ensuring children remain seated until dismissed.
  • 👀Supervision: Actively supervise the children while they eat, watching for any safety issues (spills, conflicts, or choking hazards).
  • 🙋Needs Assistance: Be the first point of contact for children who need help (e.g., retrieving dropped food, needing a napkin, or assistance with seating).
  • ➡️Flow Coordination: Coordinate with the Service Team (Role 3) to ensure only seated children are served.

Role 3: Children’s Meal Server & Communicator (The Service Team)

This role provides direct meal service and handles all necessary verbal communication and announcements via the microphone.

Key Responsibilities:

  • 🥤Setup: Place disposable cups at each spot on the children’s tables immediately before children are seated.
  • 🍞Service: Distribute pre-cut bread to the seated children.
  • 💧Pouring: Pour the pre-prepared, safe-temperature tea into the children's cups.
  • 🎤Mic Communication (Pre-Meal): Briefly and clearly announce the serving process and general rules (e.g., "Stay seated until you finish breakfast").
  • 📢Mic Communication (Dismissal): Operate the microphone to clearly announce when children are dismissed to their classes or next activities once they are finished eating.

Role 4: Adult Service & Transition Coordinator (The Transition Team)

This role manages the swift cleanup of the children’s dining area and the subsequent setup and service for the adults.

Key Responsibilities:

  • 🗑️Table Clearing: Immediately after the children have been dismissed, clear all remaining cups, napkins, and debris from the children’s tables.
  • ✨Venue Transition: Fold and stack the children’s dining tables and chairs, or relocate them to the designated storage area/adult setup area, ensuring the space is ready for the adults.
  • 🛠️Adult Setup: Set up and staff the designated adult serving station with coffee, tea, bread, and any accompanying items.
  • 🧑‍🍳Adult Service: Serve bread, tea, and coffee to the arriving adults in a friendly and efficient manner.
  • 🧹Post-Meal Cleanup: Assist the Kitchen Team (Role 1) with the final wiping down of adult serving counters and general area tidy-up.
